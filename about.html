Wayne Kline

Healthcare Data Analyst | Researcher | Public Health Advocate

About Me

I'm a data analyst and researcher who finds flow in transforming complex healthcare data into actionable insights that improve patient outcomes and system performance. With dual Master's degrees in Geography (quantitative methods) and Occupational Therapy, I bring a rare combination of statistical rigor, clinical understanding, and deep commitment to health equity.

My career has been driven by a single question: How can we use data to make healthcare more effective, accessible, and equitable? Whether building air quality forecasting models that informed regional policy, conducting geospatial analysis on climate variability, or optimizing clinical workflows in hospital systems, I've consistently sought work at the intersection of analytical depth and meaningful impact.

What I Do

I turn messy data into clear stories that drive decisions.

My expertise spans the full analytical lifecycle: extracting data from complex systems, cleaning and validating datasets with meticulous attention to quality, conducting statistical analyses to uncover patterns and relationships, and translating findings into accessible visualizations and narratives for diverse stakeholders—from clinicians to C-suite executives to policymakers.

Core Competencies

  • Statistical Analysis & Modeling: Advanced proficiency in SPSS, SQL, Excel; developing expertise in Python and R for healthcare data science
  • Healthcare Data Systems: Hands-on experience with Oracle Health/Cerner EHR databases, clinical workflow optimization, capacity management analytics
  • Research Design: Published researcher with thesis work presented at three international conferences; skilled in quantitative methods, geospatial analysis, and reproducible research practices
  • Data Quality & Governance: Natural inclination toward systematic documentation, quality assurance, and building reliable data infrastructure
  • Domain Expertise: Deep understanding of healthcare operations (clinical informatics), public health (air quality/environmental health), and accessibility/inclusive design

My Approach

I believe the best analysis combines technical precision with empathy.

My clinical training taught me to listen deeply, observe carefully, and consider the human context behind every data point. My research training taught me to question assumptions, document systematically, and communicate findings clearly. Together, these perspectives enable me to:

  • Ask better research questions that matter to real stakeholders
  • Identify data quality issues that others might miss
  • Translate technical findings for non-technical audiences
  • Design analyses that respect the complexity of healthcare systems
  • Advocate for inclusive practices and accessibility in data products
